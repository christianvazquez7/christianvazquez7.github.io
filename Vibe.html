Vibe: Digital Messages Through Physical Media



TL;DR : Taking advantage of cymatic phenomena to communicate information on our mobile devices.

A lot of our information exchange has become digital in the past decade. For instance, the physical medium of letters is no longer as dominant as the simple act of sending an email. The same applies for media, as we’ve changed from reading hardcovers to our Kindle or some other form of digital reader. In my opinion, there is some beauty in physical media that unfortunately can’t be portrayed in digital messages. But what if we could combine the digital communication with a physical representation of it? Imagine media that is sent digital and received in the physical realm. Think of cymatics, vibrational phenomena that can create patterns on the surface of a medium covered with a thin coating of particles. I believe we could take advantage of this phenomenon and represent messages in the physical world. Imagine creating a special phone cover that suspends a thin layer of granular particles between thin films that could create cymatic patterns when the phone vibrates at specific frequencies. Several playful usages come to mind, like exchanging secret messages, or identifying your caller by the symbol displayed on the back of your phone.
