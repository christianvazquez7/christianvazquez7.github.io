Contextual Games: Changing the Way We Play In the World


Contextual games are interactive experiences whose parameters evolve according to information that is specific to the player’s context. Unlike AR games, which intend to embed a virtual reality on your physical reality to augment the gaming experience, contextual games embed your physical reality within the game's parameters. This affords many experiences that are unique to the player’s individual reality. Instead of embedding static content, one can imagine a procedurally generated environment that takes full advantage of sensors on the gaming device to create a unique instance of the game’s world. Aligning the differences between the game world and the physical reality of the player can lead to an increased immersion into the game, resulting in a more enjoyable session. The embedding of contextual experiences can be passive or active in nature, depending on the role of context in the gameplay mechanics. An example of a passive contextual presence would be that the game’s time reflects the time of day of the player. This wouldn’t necessarily affect the gameplay mechanics but subtly suggests a link between both mediums. On the contrary, an active experience changes the gameplay mechanics, in such ways that the player could make use of his context to his advantage. Imagine a world in which a lake is blocking the player’s passage to the next stage. In this scenario, the player could move to a colder environment in the physical world, such that the lake could freeze, allowing the player safe passage. Such experiences afford exploration, and playing in the world instead of playing home.
